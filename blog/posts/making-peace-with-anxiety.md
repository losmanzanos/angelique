If you live with anxiety, you already know it rarely listens to logic. You can
*know* you're safe and still feel your heart racing. That gap — between what we
know and what we feel — is where so much of the struggle lives.

## Anxiety is doing a job

It helps to remember that anxiety isn't a malfunction. It's an old, protective
part of you scanning for danger and trying to keep you safe. The goal of therapy
usually isn't to delete it, but to help it relax its grip.

## Small things that help

- **Name it** — "I'm noticing anxiety right now" creates a little space between you and the feeling.
- **Slow the exhale** — a longer out-breath than in-breath signals safety to your body.
- **Get specific** — vague dread shrinks when you ask, "What exactly am I worried about?"

> You don't have to feel calm to be okay. You just have to keep taking the next
> small, kind step.

If anxiety has been running the show lately, that's worth talking through together.
