Most couples who come to therapy aren't fighting because something is broken.
They're fighting because something matters, and they haven't yet found a way to
say it that the other person can hear.

## The pattern underneath the argument

Couples tend to repeat a handful of arguments in different costumes. The topic
changes — money, in-laws, chores — but the underlying loop is often the same: one
person reaches, the other pulls back, and both end up feeling alone.

## Turning toward each other

- **Lead with the feeling, not the accusation** — "I felt forgotten" lands differently than "You never help."
- **Get curious before you get defensive** — assume there's a good reason your partner sees it their way.
- **Repair quickly** — it's not the rupture that defines a relationship, it's how fast you come back together.

> Connection isn't the absence of conflict. It's what becomes possible when
> conflict is handled with care.

If the same argument keeps coming back, couples work can help you find the
conversation underneath it.
