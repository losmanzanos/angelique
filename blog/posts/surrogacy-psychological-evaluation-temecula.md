If you're pursuing surrogacy — as an Intended Parent or as a Gestational Carrier — you've likely encountered the requirement for a psychological evaluation. It's a standard part of the process, required by most reputable fertility clinics and surrogacy agencies before any medical protocols begin.

What it actually involves, and what it's really for, is often less clear.

## What Is a Psychological Evaluation for Surrogacy?

A psychological evaluation in the context of third-party reproduction is a clinical assessment conducted by a licensed mental health professional. Its purpose is to evaluate emotional readiness, identify any psychological considerations that could affect the process, and ensure that everyone involved has a clear understanding of what lies ahead.

These evaluations are not designed to disqualify people. They're designed to support everyone's wellbeing and to surface any areas where additional preparation or support might be helpful before the process begins.

## For Intended Parents

For Intended Parents, the evaluation typically includes:

- An exploration of the emotional journey that led you to surrogacy, including any prior fertility treatments or losses
- Assessment of your understanding of the surrogacy process and the relational dynamics it involves
- Discussion of how you and your partner (if applicable) are communicating and supporting each other
- Exploration of how you're thinking about disclosure — when and how you might talk with your child about their origins

The goal isn't to test you. It's to ensure you're going into this process with clarity, and to identify any areas where additional support might strengthen your experience.

## For Gestational Carriers

For Gestational Carriers and Surrogates, the evaluation typically includes:

- Assessment of motivation and understanding of what the surrogacy journey involves
- Exploration of family and social support systems
- Discussion of the emotional dimensions of carrying a child for another family
- Review of any history relevant to emotional wellbeing during pregnancy

This is a supportive process, not a gatekeeping one. The evaluation is meant to give you space to think through what you're taking on and to ensure you have the clarity and support to do it well.

## Beyond the Evaluation: Ongoing Support

Many Intended Parents and Gestational Carriers find that the evaluation is just the beginning of what's valuable in working with a therapist during the surrogacy journey. Ongoing support can help with the emotional complexity of the waiting, the unexpected feelings that arise at various stages, and the transitions that come as the process unfolds.
